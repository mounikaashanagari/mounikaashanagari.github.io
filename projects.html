Master’s Thesis Project: SCNet-Based Deep Learning Integration of GWAS and Single-Nucleus Multiomics for Alzheimer’s Disease

This ongoing thesis project focuses on integrating Alzheimer’s Disease GWAS data with single-nucleus multiomics datasets using deep learning models.

  • Integrating Alzheimer’s Disease GWAS data with single-nucleus multiomics datasets.
  • Applying scNET deep learning models to identify cell-type-specific pathogenic gene modules.
  • Using reciprocal validation frameworks with MAGMA, CELLEX, and scDRS pipelines.
  • Training large-scale neural network models across millions of single-cell observations.
  • Investigating microglial and neuronal vulnerability pathways associated with disease risk.
  • Performing model benchmarking and validation using ROSMAP cohort data.

Biostatistics Project: Evaluation of D-Penicillamine in Primary Biliary Cirrhosis

Conducted a biostatistical evaluation comparing D-penicillamine treatment with placebo in patients with primary biliary cirrhosis.

  • Analyzed age, albumin, bilirubin levels, and disease stages.
  • Used t-tests, Chi-square tests, and regression methods.
  • Found significant differences in bilirubin levels between treatment and placebo groups.

Regression Analysis Project: Life Expectancy, Alcohol Consumption, and Adult Mortality

Investigated whether life expectancy is lower in countries with higher alcohol consumption and adult mortality after adjusting for health expenditure, BMI, and development status.

  • Used Health and Demographics data from WHO and United Nations from 2000 to 2015.
  • Analyzed 193 countries, 22 variables, and 2,938 observations.
  • Conducted descriptive statistics using SAS PROC UNIVARIATE.
  • Generated scatterplots with LOESS curves.
  • Checked outliers, data errors, and multicollinearity using VIFs.
  • Fitted multiple linear regression models using PROC REG and PROC GLM.
  • Evaluated model assumptions including linearity, homoscedasticity, normality, and multicollinearity.

Tools Used: SAS, PROC REG, PROC GLM, PROC UNIVARIATE, PROC SGSCATTER

Multivariate Analysis Project: Cardiometabolic Profile Pre- and Post-Pandemic Modified by Physical Activity

Assessed whether cardiometabolic health profiles differed before and after the COVID-19 pandemic and whether physical activity modified this association.

  • Used NHANES 2017-2018 and NHANES August 2021-August 2023 data.
  • Included 6,804 U.S. adults aged 20-70 years.
  • Analyzed systolic blood pressure, diastolic blood pressure, total cholesterol, and waist circumference.
  • Used Hotelling’s T² test, MANOVA, and MANCOVA.
  • Adjusted for age and daily caloric intake.
  • Found significant differences in overall cardiometabolic profiles before and after the pandemic.

Methods Used: NHANES data, Hotelling’s T² test, MANOVA, MANCOVA, Bonferroni confidence intervals, Wilks’ Lambda, AIC, residual diagnostics
